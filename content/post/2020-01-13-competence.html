“Labour economic policies are popular, so why aren’t Labour?”

As this analysis from YouGov suggests, Labour’s policy proposals were popular when taken one-by-one, but when voters step back and evaluate which party would do best on the economy overall, Labour is at a severe disadvantage.

Voters were twice as likely to pick Boris Johnson (34%) over Jeremy Corbyn (16%), when asked who they trusted most to run the economy.

It is no wonder, then, that in the wake of Labour’s electoral defeat, many have argued that Labour can only win if it convinces the voters that the party would be a competent steward of the economy.

LEADER IS KEY

[4] analyze results from 52 national election surveys from 13 Western European parliamentary democracies between 1974 and 2016. they show that leader effects (i.e. how much someone likes their most liked leader) have become more important to turnout, versus simple partisanship

Is competence all-important?

this study suggests that perceptions of warmth (think: trustworthiness, honesty, integrity) are actually more important than perceptions of competence in determining vote choice.

note they use survey data from the US and also a survey experiment to show causality [7]

well, surely Labour has an advantage on that??

yes, in general RW parties own competence and LW parties own character [8]

yes, but actually a very small one in 2019…

so, a plausible alternative strategy is to really double down on “nasty party” attacks and hype up warmth…

indeed, warmth is more important to left-wing voters, while strong leadership (somewhat closer to competence?) is more important to right-wing voters…

note: [10] show that a “good gut feeling” is crucial in helping low-turnout voters to overcome their ambivalence. “good gut feeling” is linked to warmth - who would you like to have a beer with.

Competence is situationally dependent!

[11] show that dominance is associated with competence in times of “conflict” - but not otherwise. show panel waves. note, this effect overwhelms policy considerations among “non-memorizers”! what does this mean? people have multiple online tallies, and retrieve different ones in different contexts - imagine we gave 10 points to hufflepuff for being smart, etc. etc.

Strategies for Labour
